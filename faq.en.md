## ğŸ“… Event Logistics

### Q: Can I stay overnight?
**A:** Yes, accommodation is available at the National Olympics Memorial Youth Center from February 7 (Sat) evening through February 11 (Wed).

### Q: Are meals provided?
**A:** Yes, breakfast, lunch, and dinner are provided for all 4 days (Venue: Cafeteria Fuji).

**Important:**
- **Meal tickets required**: You must bring meal tickets distributed at check-in
- **No reissuance**: Lost tickets cannot be reissued. Keep them safe
- **Strict timing**: Meals only available during designated times

**Meal Times:**
- **Breakfast**: 7:00-8:30 (Last order: 8:30)
- **Lunch**: 11:30-13:15 (Last order: 13:15)
- **Dinner**: 17:15-19:00 (Last order: 19:00)

**Other:**
- Please inform staff in advance if you have allergies or dietary restrictions
- No meals provided outside designated times

### Q: What should I bring?
**A:** Essential items:
- Laptop (with charger)
- Stationery
- Change of clothes and toiletries
- Health insurance card (copy acceptable)

Recommended:
- Mobile battery
- Materials/tools for presentation

### Q: How do I get to the venue?
**A:** About 7-minute walk from Sangubashi Station (Odakyu Line), or 10-minute walk from Yoyogi-koen Station (Tokyo Metro Chiyoda Line). See [Contact](contact.en.md) for details.

## ğŸ† Presentation & Awards

### Q: What is the final presentation format?
**A:** 8-minute presentation + 3-minute Q&A per team. Prepare presentation materials (PowerPoint, Google Slides, etc.) and demo (if possible).

### Q: Must presentations be in English?
**A:** **Slides must be in English**. Presentation language can be Japanese, Korean, or English. Simultaneous interpretation available via Teams.

### Q: What are the judging criteria?
**A:**
- Importance and understanding of the problem
- Innovation of the solution
- Feasibility
- Teamwork and presentation

### Q: How is prize money distributed?
**A:** Discuss within your team. Equal distribution among 4 members is common.
